01 Trend & Sub-sector

Overview of the Industry Landscape

  • Core Competitive Moat: Competition has shifted from CAR design to Gene Editing Platforms (CRISPR, TALEN). Survival depends on overcoming GvHD (safety) and HvG (durability).
  • Market Development Stage: The sector is predominantly in the Clinical Stage. It represents a high-potential blue ocean heavily backed by Venture Capital and Big Pharma partnerships.
  • Strategic Imperative: For Biopharmaceutical commercialization, clinical efficacy must be balanced with Scalable Manufacturing (CMC) to achieve viable profit margins.

Autologous vs Allogeneic CAR-T: Structural Comparison

Structural Bottlenecks of Autologous CAR-T
  • Long Production Cycle: 3-4 weeks, causing treatment delays
  • High Cost: COGS $100-150K/patient, List price $375-475K
  • Limited Capacity: Difficult to scale with individualized manufacturing
Disruptive Advantages of Allogeneic CAR-T
  • Off-the-Shelf Model: Immediate availability, no waiting time; 100+ doses per batch
  • Cost Reduction via Scale: Projected COGS $20-40K/patient, 50%-70% cost reduction
  • Indication Expansion: Applicable to solid tumors and repeat dosing scenarios

03 Demand Identification

Unmet Medical Needs of Autologous CAR-T

Treatment Window Missing
  • 41% of referred patients did not receive CAR-T therapy
  • 34% lost opportunity due to disease progression
Extremely Low Accessibility
  • 30-40% of patients cannot yield viable T cells
  • Elderly patients & multi-line treated patients are excluded
Unsustainable Costs
  • <5% of eligible patients worldwide can afford treatment
  • Total cost per patient: $700K-$1M (US)
Efficacy & Safety Gaps
  • 1-year recurrence rate: 30-50%
  • Grade 3+ CRS incidence: 5-30%

Key Demand Drivers

Epidemiology-driven

Global ageing + rising incidence of hematological malignancies; wide treatment gap in developing countries

Payment-driven

US CMS expands coverage; Chinese CAR-T added to commercial insurance; payers' value-based purchasing

Clinically-driven

Treatment line advancement (3rd→2nd→1st); 15% of patients require immediate intervention; efficacy validated

Technology-driven

CRISPR TCR knockout eliminates GvHD; iPSC-derived endless supply; China's glycosyl shield breakthrough

  • First Installment (30%): Investment agreement signed; all equity pledges released; patent litigation settled
  • Second Installment (25%): CTD402 Phase II enrollment completed; ORR/CR meets pre-defined endpoints
  • Third Installment (25%): CTA313 Phase II data readout meets target; Orphan/breakthrough therapy designation obtained
  • Fourth Installment (20%): Phase III clinical trial initiated / IND approved
Anti-dilution and Repurchase Rights
  • Full ratchet / weighted average anti-dilution provision to avoid equity dilution from low-price financing

    • Core patent invalidation / major litigation affecting pipeline
